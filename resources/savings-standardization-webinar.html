The diagnosis most enterprises miss

Decentralised buying quietly drains your margin.

Each site funds its own overhead. Each supplier charges what each site will accept. Nobody is negotiating as one customer. The numbers below come from a real ten-year programme at a global manufacturer with sites on four continents.

total saved across four standardization sub-projects in 10 years.

The agenda

What you walk out with.

Four lessons from our standardization engagements. Honest about what works, honest about what backfires.

01

Where the money actually hides

The three concrete ways decentralised buying leaks margin. Overhead duplication. Asymmetric pricing. No global negotiating power. Why none of it shows up cleanly on the P&L.

02

The four trade-offs nobody warns you about

People losing decision power. Some regions getting pricier. Slow buying processes that kill the savings. Single-vendor risk. Each one anchored with a real case from the engagement.

03

Two ways to run a standardization project

Data-heavy versus trust-based. Six months to two years versus six to twelve. When to pick which, and why one ends in board-defensible savings while the other ends in faster cash.

04

A scoping framework for your own savings

Walk out with a defensible range for one of your indirect categories. We share the questions and the rough multipliers we use in a paid scoping call.
